# Reflection: Spudnik (Module 5)

## Where I was on the workflow when Housing stopped
I stopped at the baseline step. Honestly, I didn't stop because the
project was truly finished — I stopped because I had to turn it in
and moved on to working on Spudnik.

## What's different this time
The biggest difference is my mindset, not the process itself. Housing
was a rushed project I needed to turn in — I wasn't especially
invested in it beyond finishing the assignment. Spudnik is different.
It's something I've been working toward for the past year, and I
actually care about it. That makes me want to slow down and do the
process right, instead of rushing to just get something turned in.

## What I'm watching out for
My biggest worry with Spudnik is rushing it to the point where I lean
on AI tools too much and lose track of what I'm actually doing or how
the project works. I don't just want Spudnik to exist — I want to be
able to build it and fully understand how it works, not end up with
something I can't explain or maintain myself.
